THE NUMBERS
673 million people are going to bed hungry tonight.①

Not because there isn't enough food.
Not because the planet can't produce it.

Because the math doesn't work out for them.

WE GROW ENOUGH
The Earth produces enough calories to feed 10 billion people.②

We are 8.2 billion.

So where does the food go?

Landfills. Ethanol. Livestock feed. Brand strategy.
And 1.05 billion tonnes wasted at the consumer level alone — every year.③
19% OF ALL FOOD AVAILABLE TO CONSUMERS
IS WASTED.
EVERY YEAR.
That is in addition to the 13% lost before it even reaches retail.④
While lobbyists write the rules about what "food security" even means.
MEET EVIL CORP
They don't have one name. That's the point.

They're the $5.08 billion spent lobbying the US federal government in 2025 — a new record.⑤

They're the revolving door: nearly 70% of recent FDA commissioners have gone on to work for the pharmaceutical or food industry they once regulated.⑥

They're why your food is engineered to override your satiety signals
and why the US Farm Bill subsidizes corn syrup more than vegetables.⑦
THE OTHER MACHINE
The US spent $895 billion on national defense in FY2025.⑧
That is more than the next 10 countries combined.⑨

The defense sector spent $191 million lobbying Congress in 2025⑩ —
to ensure those contracts keep renewing.

It's not a conspiracy. It's a business model.
And it's publicly filed.
THE PLANET WE HAVE
Food loss and waste alone generates 8–10% of global greenhouse gas emissions —
nearly five times the emissions from all aviation.⑪

The science on what could be done is not in dispute.
Regenerative agriculture. Reduced mono-cropping. Shorter supply chains.
Local food systems. Radically less meat.

We know what the planet needs.

The business model is what's in dispute.
